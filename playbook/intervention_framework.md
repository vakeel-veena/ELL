# Intervention Framework

## Purpose
Provide a simple and consistent way to identify which ELL participants need support, what kind of support they need, and how progress will be reviewed.

## Health Categories

### Green
Participant is on track.
Typical signals:
- Attends consistently
- Participates meaningfully
- Follows through on commitments
- Shows confidence or visibility growth
- No significant concern from pod manager or mentor

### Amber
Participant needs monitoring or light intervention.
Typical signals:
- Uneven participation
- Limited visibility
- Delayed follow-through
- Low confidence in group settings
- Emerging concern from pod manager or mentor

### Red
Participant needs immediate intervention.
Typical signals:
- Repeated missed sessions
- Very low engagement
- Little or no follow-through
- Sharp confidence drop
- Direct concern from pod manager or mentor
- Signs of disengagement from the program

## Review Questions
For every participant reviewed:
1. What are the strongest signals from the last 2–3 weeks?
2. What is the single biggest developmental gap right now?
3. What intervention will help most?
4. Who owns the intervention?
5. When will the participant be reviewed again?

## Common Intervention Types
- 1:1 coaching conversation
- Visibility opportunity
- Workshop speaking role
- Stretch assignment
- Mentoring support
- Peer leadership opportunity
- Confidence-building assignment

## Weekly Review Rule
Each weekly review should cover:
- 1 participant needing intervention
- 1 participant who is stuck or plateauing
- 1 participant showing breakout potential
